Gelle Fra

Image: Gelle Fra

Description

The Golden Lady, also known as Gëlle Fra, is a war memorial located in Luxembourg City. Created by the Luxembourgish sculptor Claus Cito, it commemorates the thousands of Luxembourgers who volunteered for service in the armed forces of the Allied Powers during both World Wars and the Korean War. The monument holds deep historical significance and serves as a tribute to those who sacrificed for their country.

Stats:

  • Design:The centrepiece of the memorial is a 21-meter-tall granite obelisk. Atop the obelisk stands a gilded bronze statue representing Nike, the goddess of victory (also known as the “Queen of Freedom” in Luxembourgish). She holds out a laurel wreath, symbolizing victory and peace.
  • Bronze Figures: At the foot of the obelisk, there are two additional bronze figures. One lies at the base of the statue, representing a Luxembourgish soldier who died in service. The other figure sits, mourning his fallen compatriot.
  • Sculptor: Claus Cito, a native Luxembourger, created the three bronze figures.
  • Inauguration: The monument was inaugurated in 1923.
  • Inscriptions: The inscriptions at the base honor Luxembourgeois soldiers who participated in World War I battles, including the Marne, Aisne, Somme, Artois, Champagne, and Verdun.
