Space Needle

Image: Space Needle

Description

Mount Everest is a peak in the Himalaya mountain range. It is located between Nepal and Tibet, an autonomous region of China. At 8,849 meters, it is considered the tallest point on Earth. In the nineteenth century, the mountain was named after George Everest, a former Surveyor General of India. The Tibetan name is Chomolungma, which means 'Mother Goddess of the World'. The Nepali name is Sagarmatha, which has various meanings.

Stats:

  • Height: 8,849 meters
  • Location: Lies on the border between Nepal and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China
  • First Summit: Achieved by New Zealander Edmund Hillary and Sherpa Tenzing Norgay in 1953
  • Summits: More than 7,600 people have reached the top as of 2017
  • Oxygen Levels: There is 66 percent less oxygen at the top of Everest than at sea level
  • Names:In Nepal, it is called Sagarmatha ("holy mother"), and in Tibet, it is known as Chomolangma ("mother of the universe")
