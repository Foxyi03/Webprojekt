Space Needle

Image: Space Needle

Description

The Space Needle is an iconic observation tower in Seattle, Washington, United States. Designed by John Graham Jr. for the 1962 Seattle World s Fair, it stands 603 feet (184 meters) tall and features a distinctive flying saucer-shaped top.

Stats:

  • Height:The Space Needle was once the tallest structure west of the Mississippi River. It stands 184 meters tall.
  • Width: It spans 42 meters.
  • Weight: The tower weighs 9,550 tons.
  • Design:Built to withstand winds of up to 320 km/h and earthquakes of up to 9.0 magnitude (as strong as the 1700 Cascadia earthquake).
  • Observation Deck: Elevators take visitors to an observation deck 160 meters above ground in just 41 seconds. From there, you can enjoy panoramic views of the downtown Seattle skyline, the Olympic and Cascade Mountains, Mount Rainier, Mount Baker, Elliott Bay, and various islands in Puget Sound.
