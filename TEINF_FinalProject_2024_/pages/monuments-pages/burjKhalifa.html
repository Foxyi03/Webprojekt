Burj Khalifa

Image: Burj Khalifa

Description

The Burj Khalifa (Arabic: برج خليفة) is an iconic skyscraper in Dubai, United Arab Emirates.

Stats:

  • Height: It stands at a total height of 829.8 meters, making it the world's tallest structure.
  • Construction: Work began in 2004, and the exterior was completed in 2009. The primary structure is reinforced concrete, with some steel originating from the Palace of the Republic in East Berlin.
  • Design: Inspired by Islamic architecture, the Y-shaped floor plan optimizes residential and hotel space. A buttressed central core and wings support its impressive height.
  • Name: Originally called the Burj Dubai, it was renamed in honor of Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the former president of the UAE.
  • Opening: The Burj Khalifa officially opened in 2010 as part of the Downtown Dubai development.
