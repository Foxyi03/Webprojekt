Mount Olympus

Image: Mount Olympus

Description

Mount Fuji is an iconic symbol of Japan and the country's tallest peak, standing at 3,776 meters. It is an active stratovolcano located on Honshu Island, near the Pacific coast, and is part of the Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park. The mountain is renowned for its symmetrical cone shape and is a popular subject in Japanese art and culture. Despite its serene appearance, Mount Fuji sits on the junction of three tectonic plates and has a complex volcanic history. The summit crater consists of eight peaks, with Kengamine Peak being the highest point

Stats:

  • Height: 3,776.24 meters
  • Location: Honshu Island, Japan, straddling the border of Yamanashi and Shizuoka prefectures
  • First Summit: By monk En no Odzunu in 663 AD
  • Last Eruption: 1707, classified as active but dormant
  • Hike Difficulty: Moderate to challenging
  • Type of Volcano:Stratovolcano (composite volcano)
