Mont Blanc

Image: Mont Blanc

Description

Mont Blanc, also known as Monte Bianco (meaning "white mountain" in French and Italian), is the highest mountain in the Alps and Western Europe.

Stats:

  • Height: 4,805.59 meters
  • Location:It straddles the French-Italian border and is situated on the watershed line between the valleys of Ferret and Veny in Italy, and the valleys of Montjoie and Arve in France.
  • Prominence: It is the second-most prominent mountain in Europe, after Mount Elbrus, and the 11th most prominent mountain in the world.
  • Massif: Mont Blanc gives its name to the Mont Blanc massif, which spans parts of France, Italy, and Switzerland.
